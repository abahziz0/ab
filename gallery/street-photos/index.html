Street Photography — Autumn 2023

November 12, 2023
photographystreetautumn

A quiet Saturday in early November. I walked about 8km with no particular route, just turning wherever it looked interesting.

Notes on the shots

Most of these were taken in the late afternoon window — about 3pm to 5pm — when the light in autumn sits low and warm even when it's overcast. I wasn't chasing "golden hour" specifically, just trying to stay out long enough for the light to change.

What worked: leading lines, shop reflections, people caught mid-motion who didn't notice me. I find the shots where nothing much is happening are often the ones worth keeping.

What didn't: any attempt to be subtle about lifting the phone. You'd think people would be used to it by now, but the framing gesture is always obvious. I need to practice shooting from the hip more.

Gear

Phone camera only. I've been resisting carrying anything heavier because the constraint forces you to make decisions faster. You can't bracket exposures or swap lenses — you either get the frame or you don't.

Post-processing in Lightroom Mobile, usually just:

  • Pull highlights
  • Lift shadows slightly
  • Cool the white balance a degree or two
  • Add a very light vignette

The best camera is the one you have with you — and the one you'll actually use.
